Reason for exclusion

Review article.

Study about a multifactorial intervention in patients with type 2 diabetes.

Gupta 2005

In this study, oxidative stress as measured by the levels of malondialdehyde, superoxide dismutase, glutathione peroxidase and vitamin C was measured in 50 normal controls, 40 diabetics without complications, 22 diabetics with proliferative and 20 with nonproliferative retinopathy respectively.

Ivanisevic 1994

Review article.

Jennings 1987

Investigated ascorbic and dehydroascorbic acids concentrations in diabetics and matched normal controls.

Juhl 2004

The study describes a reduction in transcapillary escape rate of albumin in type‐1 diabetes patients treated with vitamin C.

Kowluru 2001

Review article.

Leal 2005

Review article.

Mamputu 2004

Authors examined the in vitro effect of advanced glycosylated endproducts on human monocyte adhesion to bovine endothelial cells and the molecular mechanisms involved in this effect.

Mayer‐Davis1998

Epidemiological study.

Millen 2003

Epidemiological study.

Newport 2005

Study about age‐related macular degeneration.

Palmieri 2006

Review article.

Porta 2004

Review article.

Rema 1995

Epidemiological study.

Sinclair 1992

This study measured free radical activity using thiobarbituric acid, malonyldialdehyde and reduced glutathione.

Soro‐Paavonen 2006

Review article.

Vinson 1994

Study investigated the beneficial effects of vitamin C and E upon sorbitol, glycation and lipid peroxidation.

Wang 2005

Review article.

Wegewitz 2005

Review article.

Yam 2007

Review article.

Yildirim 2007

Study investigated the correlation of serum copper, zinc, nitric oxide, glutathione, advanced oxidation protein products and superoxide dismutase with diabetic retinopathy severity.
