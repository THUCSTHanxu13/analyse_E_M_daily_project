Armstrong 2001

Methods

Randomised controlled trial.

Participants

Number: 75 US, community‐based patients with diabetes and a plantar foot ulcer.
25 patients in each group.
Inclusion criteria: VPT > 25 volts.
At least one palpable foot pulse or transcutaneous oximetry > 40 mmHg; diabetes‐related neuropathic plantar foot ulcer Texas Wound Classification A1.
Exclusion criteria: active infection; unable to walk; heel, rear foot wounds or in location other than plantar; severe peripheral vascular disease (absence of foot pulse on affected limb).
Baseline comparability: sex, duration of diabetes, TcPO2, wound size, wound duration, VPT.

Interventions

Group 1. total contact cast (TCC, non‐removable cast);
Group 2. removable cast walker (RCW, Aircast Diabetic Walker, removable device);
Group 3. half shoe (Darco, temporary therapeutic half shoe).

TCCs were applied using a modified technique and changed on a weekly basis or as clinically necessary. RCWs and half shoes were applied using directions supplied. All patients were instructed to use the device at all times during ambulation and the device was inspected weekly. All wounds were debrided as required on each visit.

Outcomes

Complete epithelialisation or at 12 weeks which ever came first.

Notes

Largest wound used as index ulcer.
Funding: Quote: "This study was funded by the US Department of Veterans Affairs' Rehabilitation R&D Merit Award Grant A2150RC and the Aircast Research Foundation".

Comment: It is possible that funding from the organisation which produces the pressure relieving devices could have influenced the outcomes.

Armstrong 2005

Methods

Randomised controlled trial.

Participants

Number: 50 US, community‐based patients with diabetes and a plantar foot ulcer.
27 patients in group 1 and 23 patients in group 2.
Inclusion criteria: VPT > 25 volts; at least one palpable foot pulse; neuropathic plantar diabetic foot ulcer Texas Wound Classification A1.
Exclusion criteria: active infection; unable to walk; heel, rear foot wounds or in location other than plantar; severe peripheral vascular disease (absence of foot pulse on affected limb).
Baseline comparability for age, sex, BMI, wound size, VPT, and HbA1c.

Interventions

Group 1: removable cast walker (RCW, Active Offloading Walker, removable device);
Group 2: instant total contact cast (iTCC, Active Offloading Walker, non‐removable device).

iTCC was an RCW wrapped entirely in a cohesive bandage creating an 'instant' non‐removable cast. All patients were instructed to use the device at all times during ambulation and the device was inspected weekly. All wounds were debrided as required on each visit.

Outcomes

Complete epithelialisation or at 12 weeks.
Secondary outcomes: infection; skin maceration.

Notes

Largest ulcer used as index ulcer.
Funding: Quote: "Supported by the US Department of Veterans Affairs, Health Services Research and Development Award IIR 20‐059 and the Rehabilitation Research and Development Merit Award A2150RC".

Comment: It is unlikely that funding from this organisation could have influenced the outcomes.

Exclusion criteria: peripheral vascular disease with ABPI < 0.9; clinical signs of infection; previous ulcers at same site in last 6 months; osteomyelitis; Charcot’s neuropathy; bilateral ulceration; serum creatinine > 2 mg/dl; systemic pathology or therapy interfering with healing process; severe visual or motor impairment putting patient at risk of accidents during the study and/or less than 1 year life expectancy.
Baseline comparability: age, duration of diabetes, neuropathy, area of lesion.

Interventions

Group 1: total contact cast (TCC, non‐removable cast);
Group 2: Optima Diab Walker (removable device).

Ulcers were surgically debrided in both groups removing all non‐viable tissue, exposing the ulcer. Ulcers were dressed with gauze containing paraffin and covered with a single layer of sterile gauze. In group 1 the TCC was then applied with the ulcer protected by a layer of foam. In Group 2 the Optima Diab Walker was applied to each patient and individually adapted. Cotton wool was used to protect the limb and the three‐layer insole adapted to accommodate the ulcer.

Outcomes

Complete re‐epithelialisation at 12 weeks.
Secondary outcomes: infection; cost.

Methods

Participants

Number: 43 patients with diabetes and a neuropathic plantar foot ulcer.
23 patients in group 1 and 20 patients in group 2.
Inclusion criteria: sensory neuropathy threshold 10 g monofilament; Wagner Grade 1 or 2 ulcer.
Exclusion criteria: patients unable to walk indoors; dementia; life threatening co‐morbidity; ABPI < 0.4 and/or osteomyelitis.

Baseline comparability: imbalance for HbA1c and gender in both groups, but this would be unlikely to affect the results.

One participant crossed over from TCC to CTF group, but was analysed in TCC group.

Interventions

Group 1: total contact cast (TCC, non‐removable cast);
Group 2: custom‐made temporary footwear (temporary therapeutic shoe).

Group 1 patients had a TCC applied over a single layer of padding applied following debridement and application of a moisture retentive dressing. The cast was changed weekly. Group 2 patients received a custom‐made shoe comprising a rigid leather socket with rubber to restrict movement and cork insoles to allow pressure relief for the ulcer. All patients were given instructions regarding routine care and advised to restrict their mobility to one third of that pretrial.

Outcomes

Complete re‐epithelialisation at 16 weeks:
Secondary outcomes: amputation; concordance; mortality.
