Abbatecola 2006

Methods

Study design: randomised controlled trial.

Unit of allocation: by individuals.

Start date: September 2001 (initial recruitment).

End date: September 2004 (final recruitment).

Duration of participation: 12 months.

Participants

Participants: 156 older people with Type 2 diabetes (age range 60 to 78 years) volunteered for the study. Participants were selected from a group of approximately 250 individuals with diagnosed Type 2 diabetes from university outpatient offices. Physicians were asked to refer older persons with Type 2 diabetes who had not previously undergone any antidiabetic drug treatment to their offices where further selection was performed.

Population description: not reported.

Setting: academic centre.

Method of recruitment of participants: recruited from outpatient university clinics and referred to the study office.

Total number randomised: 156.

Baseline imbalances: no significant differences were found between study groups.

Age: glibenclamide (glyburide) group = 74.3 (SD 2.3) years; repaglinide group = 74.5 (SD 2.5) years.

Sex: glibenclamide (glyburide) group = 41 female/38 male; repaglinide group = 39 female/38 male.

Race (white): not reported.

BMI: glibenclamide (glyburide) group = 26.7 (SD 0.4); repaglinide group = 27.1 (SD 0.2).

Glycated haemoglobin, standardised level‐%

Glibenclamide (glyburide) group = 7.2 (SD 0.7).

Repaglinide group = 7.3 (SD 0.8).

Interventions

Types of intervention: glibenclamide (glyburide) initially started with 2.5 mg twice a day, given up to 30 minutes before daily meals.

Types of comparison: repaglinide initially started with 1 mg twice a day, given up to 30 minutes before daily meals.

To achieve target glycaemic control, titration adjustments were made according to World Health Organization recommendations over an initial 3‐week period prior to the beginning of study protocol, but no data regarding the increasing doses were found in the reference given by the author in the study about this point.

Outcomes

Global cognitive function: MMSE.

Composite outcome:'Cognition Composite Score' (attention and executive function)

TMT‐A, TMT‐B, TMT‐B minus TMT‐A (DIFF B‐A), Wechsler Adult Intelligence Scale–Revised Digit Span: Backward Digit Span (DSP–Backward), Wechsler Adult Intelligence Scale–Revised Digit Span: Forward Digit Span (DSP–Forward), Verbal Fluency Test.

Notes

Sample size calculation: sample size calculation, performed with an alfa = 0.05 and a beta = 0.80, was found to be at least 110 subjects in order to prove a significant decline of 3% to 4% in the MMSE at the 12th month.

Funding: funded by the Second University of Naples (fiscal year 2004).

Unclear risk

Comments: no methods for allocation concealment have been described.

All outcomes
